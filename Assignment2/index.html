Global Plastic Waste

Introduction

Waste is a product of our lives. It is a waste product that has lost its use value and cannot be used, it is an important part of material recycling. In some big cities, some type of waste disposal is a problem, for example, plastic.

The common way is to collect and send the waste for landfill disposal, or to incinerate them in incinerators. But both create environmental concerns. Waste disposal in landfills pollutes groundwater and emits odors, and in many cities there is less space available for landfills. And incineration inevitably produces toxic gases .

Marine debris

So what to do with the discarded plastic waste? This discarded waste will send to the ocean and becomes marine debris. Plastics are difficult to biodegrade and form an important part of marine debris. Some of these human-made and refining wastes stay on the beach, and some float on the ocean surface or sink to the ocean.

The map on the right shows the percentage of plastic waste the world sends to the ocean. It can be seen that although countries with high GDP such as the United States and Germany are relatively ahead in the production of plastic waste, the rate of emitted plastic waste to the ocean is indeed very low. But this is not because these countries have good waste disposal technology, they export waste to other countries, specialy Asian countries, such as China. However, China has stopped accepting plastic waste imports since 2017.

Waste find in River and Ocean

Marine debris is poisoning the ocean and the species that live rely on ocean. Many news reports of marine life deaths, starvation and drowning due to accidental ingestion of plastic debris. The most common types of plastic waste in the ocean are listed in the radial plot on the left. And this plastic waste will also end up in the seafood we eat and hide in our body.

The bags (14.06% of the total waste found) are the most common waste found in the ocean and river , while plastic bottles are the second most waste (11.89%). Drink canns were not found that common, ranking last (3.17%) in the top 10 most common waste find in river and ocean.

Marine Debris Decomposition Time

It takes many years for this marine debris to decomposition into the ocean. The graph on the right shows how long it takes for some of the most common marine debris to decomposition in the ocean. The fishing lines taking the longest to decomposition, which is 600 years. The decomposition time of plastic bottle, disposable diaper and plastic beverage also hold longer than 400 years.

A large amount of marine debris has accumulated because it takes too long to decompose. The marine debris in the Pacific Ocean alone has reached more than 3 million square kilometers, even more than the land area of India. A huge plastic-dominated "garbage patch" has formed in the Pacific Ocean. If we don't do somthing, the oceans will no longer be able to keep up with the vast amount of marine debris, and we are not gointg to survive either if we lost our ocean. We should strengthen public awareness of environmental protection and reduce the use of plastics and single-use products as much as possible.
